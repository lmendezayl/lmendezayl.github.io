[04/04/2025] Hard work pays, sometimes

The moment I saw my exam result, I actually had to stare at it for a few seconds to make sure I wasn’t imagining things. After all those weeks of grinding, panicking, and convincing myself I was going to fail, seeing that passing grade felt surreal. A seven, handwritten where I expected a number lower than 5. I didn’t celebrate right away—I just sat there, exhausted, relieved, and a little stunned that the thing I’d been dreading for weeks was finally over, and I’d made it through.

 

The lead-up was brutal. There were days when I’d sit at my desk for hours, flipping through hand notes, vectors, matrices equations, rewriting concepts; only to feel like none of it was sticking. I'd practice questions and get half of them wrong, and that voice in my head would whisper, “You’re not cut out for this”. “What if you’re just wasting your time?” I’d seen classmates breeze through material that left me stuck, and it made me wonder if I was missing something, if I was somehow falling behind without realizing it.

 

But I kept going anyway, even when it felt pointless. I stopped trying to memorize everything at once and just focused on one thing at a time. I let myself take breaks without guilt, because I finally accepted that staring at notes for ten hours straight wasn’t helping if my brain was fried. And when the exam day came, I walked through the gates, fully expecting to blank every question—but somehow, the work I’d put in carried me through.

 

And through all of it, there was one person who never let me drown in my own doubts—my girlfriend. She was the one who brought me coffee at any hour when I was still hunched over my books, who listened to me rant about how impossible it all felt, and who somehow always knew when to push me and when to just hug me and tell me it would be okay. I don’t think I could’ve kept my sanity through this without her.

 

Passing didn’t just feel like luck. It felt like proof that even when you’re convinced you’re doomed, even when every practice test feels like a disaster, you can still surprise yourself—especially when you have someone in your corner who believes in you more than you believe in yourself.

 

I get it. It’s exhausting. But keep going. You might be closer than you think.
